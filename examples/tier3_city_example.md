### Core vs Non-Core Differentiation

- Core Area: May retain some self-use value if schools, hospitals, jobs, and scarce services are concentrated.
- Mature Urban Area: Selective stability possible, but liquidity and resale demand must be checked.
- Outer Suburb: High risk if commute is long and listings are rising.
- New District: Highest inventory and population-import risk; avoid treating planning promises as actual demand.
- Satellite City: Vulnerable to weak local employment and commuting friction.

### Self-Use Recommendation

- Recommendation: Only selective and needs strong personal-use reason.
- Reason: A core-area home may be acceptable for family stability if affordability is comfortable and holding period is long.
- Suitable Buyer Type: Local household with stable employment, low leverage, and clear school/commute/service needs.

### Investment Recommendation

- Recommendation: Not recommended except rare core scarce assets with verified liquidity.
- Reason: Population outflow, weak industry, high inventory, and low rental yield reduce investment attractiveness.
- Leverage Warning: Avoid leverage based on policy easing; falling liquidity can amplify loss.

### Risks and Data Gaps

- Main Risks: Population outflow, high inventory, weak industry, discounted new-home sales, missing verified latest transactions.
- Data Gaps: Latest transaction volume, listing volume, rental yield, district inventory, and population flow data are missing in this example.
- Notes on conflicting signals: Policy easing may reduce short-term pressure, but it does not override weak demand and inventory pressure.
