# Risk Rules

Use these rules to prevent overconfident or one-factor conclusions.

## Core Prohibitions

1. Do not judge price growth only from population inflow.
2. Do not judge all areas as falling only from population outflow.
3. Do not judge housing recovery only from lower interest rates.
4. Do not ignore the impact of economic downturns on income expectations and purchase confidence.
5. Do not equate city-wide population growth with core-area home-buying demand.
6. Do not equate short-term employment or policy stimulus with long-term fundamental improvement.
7. Do not treat policy easing as effective unless transaction or price behavior confirms it.
8. Do not ignore inventory pressure in outer suburbs, new districts, and weak new-home markets.
9. Do not hide district divergence behind city-wide averages.
10. Do not make high-confidence forecasts when key data is missing.
11. Do not treat asking prices as transaction prices.
12. Do not treat new-home filing prices as real market prices.
13. Do not treat discounted new-home sales as organic recovery.
14. Do not give investment-positive conclusions when rental yield is very low and liquidity is weak.
15. Do not give high-confidence forecasts if transaction and inventory data are missing.
16. Do not merge self-use and investment recommendations.
17. Do not omit the standard disclaimer from formal outputs: 仅供交流学习娱乐，不提供投资建议，所有解释权归作者所有。

## Fresh Data Risk Rules

18. Do not claim to have used latest data unless source and date are shown.
19. Do not present stale data as current.
20. Do not rely only on search snippets if the underlying source is unavailable.
21. Do not use social media sentiment as a core data source.
22. Do not use platform asking prices as verified transaction prices.
23. Do not treat a single district or project as representative of the whole city.
24. Do not ignore data freshness when making short-term forecasts.
25. Do not give high-confidence 3-month or 6-month forecasts without recent transaction or listing data.
26. Do not infer policy effectiveness from policy text alone; require transaction, credit, or price confirmation.
